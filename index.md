I am a computer scientist at the [Australian National University](https://comp.anu.edu.au), working at the intersection of formal methods, programming languages, and concurrency. My research aims to transform how software systems are built—using mathematical proof to provide strong guarantees of correctness and security.

My work addresses real-world challenges, from the first rigorous formal analysis of the AODV routing protocol—which uncovered critical flaws contradicting the official standard—to the co-design and formal verification of communication protocols within DARPA’s High-Assurance Cyber Military Systems program. As part of this effort, I contributed to a team recognised with DARPA’s prestigious [Game Changer Award](https://www.darpa.mil/news-events/2023-01-24) in 2023.

Before joining ANU in 2020, I spent nearly a decade as a (Principal) Research Scientist at [Data61, CSIRO](https://www.data61.csiro.au) (formerly NICTA), with concurrent appointments as Conjoint Associate Professor at UNSW and Honorary Senior Research Fellow at Macquarie University. Earlier in my career I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Augsburg, Germany.
